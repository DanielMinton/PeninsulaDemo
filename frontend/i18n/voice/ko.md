# Korean (ko) — voice guide

The Korean-speaking population on the SF Peninsula is heavily concentrated in Santa Clara County and parts of San Mateo County. Readers are predominantly first- and 1.5-generation Korean-Americans plus more recent immigrants. The voice should read as warm and respectful — neighborly, not corporate.

## Tone

Warm, plainspoken, confident. Avoid the stiff written-business register (격식체 / 합쇼체) — that reads as a press release, not a small family business. Reach for **해요체** (the friendly polite form) throughout the body copy. Reserve **합쇼체** only for legal text and disclaimers where formality signals seriousness (e.g. consent text on the quote form).

## Address form

- Refer to the customer as **고객님** (gohaek-nim — esteemed customer) in form labels and CTAs. **손님** (sonnim — guest) reads warmer in body copy.
- Imperatives in 해요체: "전화 주세요" (please call), not "전화하십시오". Soften with **부탁드려요** (we ask of you) only when the action is significant — e.g. consent.
- First-person plural for the business: **저희** (jeohi — humble we). Always 저희, never 우리, when the business is the subject.

## Vocabulary

- **"Junk removal"** → **쓰레기 수거** (sseureghi sugeo — waste collection) is the most common term. **폐기물 처리** (pyegimul cheori — disposal of waste) is more formal/industrial. Use 쓰레기 수거 for general copy.
- **"Hauling"** → **운반** (unban — carrying/hauling) for general; **이송** (isong) for goods-specific.
- **"Cleanout"** → **정리** (jeongri — tidying) is gentle; **대청소** (daecheongso — big cleanup) connotes whole-property scale.
- **"Pickup"** as an action → **수거** (sugeo — collection). Brand name "Peninsula Pick Ups" stays English.
- **"Free quote"** → **무료 견적** (muryo gyeonjeok — free estimate). Standard.
- **"Same-day service"** → **당일 서비스** (dangil seobiseu).
- **"Family-owned"** → **가족이 운영하는** (gajogi unyeonghaneun — family-operated). Avoid 가족 회사 (sounds dynastic).
- **"Licensed and insured"** → **정식 면허 및 보험 가입** (jeongsik myeonheo mit boheom gaip — proper license + insurance enrolled).

## What to avoid

- **합쇼체 stiffness in marketing copy** — "전화하시기 바랍니다" reads as government PSA. Use 전화 주세요 instead.
- **Excessive Hanja (Sino-Korean) compound words** in a small-business context. Native Korean readings warm the register.
- **Romanization of brand names** beyond what's necessary. "Peninsula Pick Ups" stays in Roman letters; do not write "페닌슐라 픽업스" — Korean readers handle Roman business names fluently.
- **English-style quotation marks** ("smart" curly quotes). Korean uses 「」 or "" depending on style; for inline quoting in body copy, "..." is acceptable.

## Trust phrases (locked translations)

- "Licensed and insured" → 정식 면허 및 보험 가입
- "Family owned" → 가족이 운영하는
- "Locally owned" → 지역에서 운영되는
- "Verified business line" → 인증된 사업자 전화번호
- "Free quote" → 무료 견적

## Phone numbers and brand names

- Phone number `(650) 201-1543` renders LTR exactly as `(650) 201-1543`. Never converted to Korean number-words.
- "Peninsula Pick Ups" stays in English. Owner names "Don" and "Melissa" stay in English.
- City names: keep English ("San Carlos, CA"). Optional Hangul gloss on first mention is unnecessary for Bay Area readers.
