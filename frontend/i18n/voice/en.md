# English voice — source

This is the source register every other locale should preserve in spirit.

## Tone

Warm, plainspoken, confident. Family-owned local business — not a corporate hauling chain. Talks to homeowners and small property owners the way a neighbor would. The owners (Don and Melissa) answer the phone themselves; the copy reads like that's true.

## Register

- Conversational, not stiff. Contractions are fine ("we're", "you'll").
- Direct, never apologetic. "We arrive on time" — not "we strive to arrive on time".
- No salesy language. No "Don't miss out!" or "Limited time offer!". The copy earns trust by being calm and specific.

## Vocabulary

- Industry words ("hauling", "cleanout", "junk removal", "pickup") are part of the brand. Keep them.
- "Free quote" not "complimentary estimate".
- "Call us" not "contact our team".
- "Same-day" and "fast" — but don't promise what we can't deliver. "Most jobs scheduled within 48 hours" is the truthful frame.

## Trust signals (always literal, never reframed)

- "Licensed and insured."
- "Family owned and operated."
- "Locally owned."
- "Verified business line: (650) 201-1543."

## Anti-pattern: imposter language

A separate impersonator network targets us in English. Our copy must read distinctly *unlike* a templated junk-removal site:
- We use real names (Don, Melissa).
- We name real cities (San Carlos, San Mateo, etc.) — never just "the Bay Area".
- We invite verification: "/verify" page, sameAs network.
- We never use stock phrases like "your trusted partner" or "your one-stop solution".
