# Dutch (nl) — voice guide

Standard Netherlands Dutch (Algemeen Nederlands). Avoid Belgian/Flemish-only vocabulary. The Dutch service-business voice is **direct, clear, no-nonsense** — Dutch readers prefer practical specifics over marketing rhetoric.

## Tone

Direct, friendly, professional. Dutch culture values concision and honesty in business communication; flowery marketing language reads as untrustworthy. The voice should be confident without overstatement.

## Address form

- Use **u** (formal "you") consistently for marketing-to-customer copy. Switching to **je/jij** (informal) is too familiar from a service business addressing strangers.
- Refer to ourselves as **wij** (we) or simply implied subject. "Wij regelen het" — we handle it.
- Imperatives are direct: **bel** (call), **vraag aan** (request). No softening particles needed.

## Vocabulary

- **"Junk removal"** → **afvalverwijdering** (waste removal) is the standard term. **grofvuil ophalen** (bulky waste pickup) is the conventional service description.
- **"Hauling"** → **transport** or **vervoer** (transport). For the trade-specific term: **wegtransport**.
- **"Cleanout"** → **leegruimen** (clearing out) for properties; **opruimen** for general cleanup.
- **"Pickup"** as a service action → **ophalen** (pickup, fetching). Brand "Peninsula Pick Ups" stays English.
- **"Free quote"** → **gratis offerte** (free quotation). Standard.
- **"Same-day service"** → **service op dezelfde dag**.
- **"Family-owned"** → **familiebedrijf** (family business). Direct, warm.
- **"Licensed and insured"** → **gecertificeerd en verzekerd** (certified and insured).

## What to avoid

- **Belgian-Dutch (Flemish) vocabulary**: avoid "u" with verb-second word order patterns specific to Belgium ("wat denkt u?" is fine in both, but constructions like "ik heb dat gezegd geweest" are Flemish). Stick to Netherlands-standard.
- **English calques**: "Krijg een gratis offerte" reads as translated-English. "Vraag een gratis offerte aan" is natural Dutch.
- **Over-formality**. "Wenst u dat wij u contact opnemen?" sounds like a courtroom. Use "Bel ons" or "Neem contact op."
- **Marketing hyperbole**. "INCREDIBLE!" energy doesn't work in Dutch. Specifics build trust.

## Trust phrases (locked translations)

- "Licensed and insured" → Gecertificeerd en verzekerd
- "Family owned" → Familiebedrijf
- "Locally owned" → Lokaal bedrijf
- "Verified business line" → Geverifieerd zakelijk telefoonnummer
- "Free quote" → Gratis offerte
