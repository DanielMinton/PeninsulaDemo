# Russian (ru) — voice guide

The Russian-speaking population in San Mateo County is concentrated in Burlingame and parts of San Mateo, with many longstanding multi-generational community members plus more recent immigrants. The voice should read as warm and direct — a small family business, not a corporate vendor.

## Tone

Warm, plainspoken, professional. Russian has a sharp register split between formal and informal — for marketing to potential customers, the **formal-but-friendly** middle ground is correct. Avoid both Soviet-era bureaucratic phrasing (which reads as cold government-speak) and overly familiar tone (which reads as untrustworthy from a service business).

## Address form

- Use **Вы** (formal "you") consistently throughout — capitalized when addressing the customer directly. Switching to **ты** (informal) is inappropriate for a service business addressing strangers, even in California's casual culture.
- Refer to the business as **мы** (we) — never the corporate **наша компания** in body copy. "Мы вывезем мусор" is right; "Наша компания осуществляет вывоз отходов" is wrong-register.
- Imperatives use the polite plural: **позвоните** (please call), **закажите** (please order), **напишите** (please write).

## Vocabulary

- **"Junk removal"** → **вывоз мусора** (vyvoz musora — removal of trash) is the standard service term. **уборка** (uborka — cleaning) is broader; **утилизация** (utilizatsiya — utilization/disposal) is too clinical for marketing.
- **"Hauling"** → **перевозка** (perevozka — transport) is general; **грузоперевозки** (gruzoperevozki) for cargo specifically.
- **"Cleanout"** → **расчистка** (raschistka — clearing out) for full-property; **уборка** for routine.
- **"Pickup"** as a service action → **вывоз** (vyvoz — removal/pickup). Keep "Peninsula Pick Ups" verbatim.
- **"Free quote"** → **бесплатная оценка** (besplatnaya otsenka — free estimate). Or **бесплатный расчёт стоимости** (free price calculation) for slightly more formal contexts.
- **"Same-day service"** → **выезд в тот же день** (vyezd v tot zhe den' — departure the same day).
- **"Family-owned"** → **семейный бизнес** (semeynyy biznes — family business). Warm, clear.
- **"Licensed and insured"** → **с лицензией и страховкой** (s litsenziey i strakhovkoy — with license and insurance).

## What to avoid

- **Soviet-era officialese** like "осуществляем", "производим", "оказываем услуги по". Use direct verbs: вывозим, забираем, делаем.
- **English calques** like "получите бесплатное предложение" (literal "get a free offer") — say "позвоните для бесплатной оценки" instead.
- **Aggressive marketing tone** — Russian readers are skeptical of pushy ads. Calm and specific beats loud and superlative.
- **Overly anglicized loanwords** when good Russian equivalents exist. E.g. сервис is fine but клиент-ориентированный подход is corporate-speak; use внимательное отношение к клиентам.
- **English brand transliteration**. "Peninsula Pick Ups" stays in Roman letters; do NOT write "Пенинсула Пик Апс."

## Trust phrases (locked translations)

- "Licensed and insured" → С лицензией и страховкой
- "Family owned" → Семейный бизнес
- "Locally owned" → Местная компания
- "Verified business line" → Проверенный номер для связи
- "Free quote" → Бесплатная оценка
