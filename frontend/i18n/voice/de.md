# German (de) — voice guide

Standard German (Hochdeutsch). Avoid Austrian-only or Swiss-German vocabulary. The German service-business voice is **clear, formal-but-friendly, precise** — Germans expect specifics in commercial copy. Vague claims read as untrustworthy.

## Tone

Confident, professional, precise. German culture values clarity and competence in business communication. Concrete details (numbers, hours, certifications) build trust faster than warm-fuzzy language.

## Address form

- Use **Sie** (formal "you", always capitalized in this context). Switching to **du** is inappropriate for a service business addressing strangers.
- Refer to ourselves as **wir** (we). Direct subject preferred over the corporate **unser Unternehmen**.
- Imperatives use the polite plural-Sie form: **rufen Sie an** (please call), **fordern Sie an** (please request).

## Vocabulary

- **"Junk removal"** → **Sperrmüllentsorgung** (bulky waste disposal) is the precise term. **Müllentsorgung** is general waste removal. Use **Entrümpelung** for whole-property clearance contexts.
- **"Hauling"** → **Transport** (general); **Abtransport** for specifically removing things.
- **"Cleanout"** → **Entrümpelung** (clearing out) — strong, native, unambiguous.
- **"Pickup"** as a service action → **Abholung** (pickup, fetching). Brand "Peninsula Pick Ups" stays English.
- **"Free quote"** → **Kostenloses Angebot** (free offer/quote). Standard.
- **"Same-day service"** → **Service am gleichen Tag**.
- **"Family-owned"** → **Familienbetrieb** (family operation). Strong, warm, traditional.
- **"Licensed and insured"** → **Lizenziert und versichert**.

## What to avoid

- **Austrian-only vocabulary**: avoid "Spital" (hospital — DE: Krankenhaus), "Marille" (apricot — DE: Aprikose), Austrian-specific business terms.
- **Anglicisms where good German exists**. "Service" is fine but "Customer Care" sounds out of place. Use "Kundenbetreuung."
- **Excessive Substantivierung** (noun-heavy bureaucratic style). "Die Durchführung der Entsorgung" → "Wir entsorgen."
- **DDR-era construction phrasing** in service context. Modern marketing register only.
- **Over-formality**. "Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren" is correct in formal letters but reads as cold for landing-page copy. "Hallo" or direct address works.

## Trust phrases (locked translations)

- "Licensed and insured" → Lizenziert und versichert
- "Family owned" → Familienbetrieb
- "Locally owned" → Lokales Unternehmen
- "Verified business line" → Verifizierte Geschäftsnummer
- "Free quote" → Kostenloses Angebot

## Phone numbers and brand names

- Phone number `(650) 201-1543` renders LTR exactly as `(650) 201-1543`. Never reformatted to German conventions.
- "Peninsula Pick Ups" stays in English. Owner names "Don" and "Melissa" stay in English.
