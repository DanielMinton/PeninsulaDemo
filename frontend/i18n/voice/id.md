# Indonesian (id) — voice guide

Bahasa Indonesia is the standardized national language. Use **bahasa baku** (standard register) for marketing copy — clear, polite, neutral. Avoid heavy slang or regional Jakarta-Indonesian (Jakartan slang reads as informal-internet, wrong for a service business).

## Tone

Warm, direct, polite. Indonesian service-business voice is approachable without being aggressive. Confidence beats hyperbole.

## Address form

- Refer to the customer as **Anda** (formal "you", capitalized when in marketing copy as a sign of respect). Never **kamu** (informal) — too familiar.
- Refer to ourselves as **kami** (exclusive we — we, not including you). "Kami melayani Anda" — we serve you.
- Imperatives are direct: **hubungi kami** (contact us), **pesan sekarang** (order now). No "tolong" softener required for service CTAs.

## Vocabulary

- **"Junk removal"** → **pengangkutan barang bekas** (transport of used items) is closest. **pengambilan sampah** is more like trash collection. Use pengangkutan barang bekas for the core service; "sampah besar" (bulky waste) works for emphasis.
- **"Hauling"** → **pengangkutan** (transport, hauling).
- **"Cleanout"** → **pembersihan total** (total cleaning) for whole-property; **pembersihan** for general.
- **"Pickup"** as a service action → **penjemputan** (pickup, fetching). Brand name "Peninsula Pick Ups" stays English.
- **"Free quote"** → **penawaran harga gratis** (free price offering).
- **"Same-day service"** → **layanan di hari yang sama**.
- **"Family-owned"** → **bisnis keluarga** (family business). Direct, warm.
- **"Licensed and insured"** → **berlisensi dan diasuransikan**.

## What to avoid

- **Heavy English code-switching** — unlike Filipino, Indonesian readers prefer cleaner Bahasa Indonesia in marketing. Loanwords are fine where they're conventional ("layanan", "klien"), but avoid forcing English where good Indonesian exists.
- **Overly formal Bahasa baku** that sounds bureaucratic. "Mohon dengan hormat" reads as government letter, not a friendly business.
- **Dutch/Old-spelling residue**: avoid "oe" for "u" (it's a pre-1972 spelling). Modern: "buku", not "boekoe."

## Trust phrases (locked translations)

- "Licensed and insured" → Berlisensi dan diasuransikan
- "Family owned" → Bisnis keluarga
- "Locally owned" → Bisnis lokal
- "Verified business line" → Nomor bisnis terverifikasi
- "Free quote" → Penawaran harga gratis
