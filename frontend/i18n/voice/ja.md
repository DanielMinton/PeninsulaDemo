# Japanese (ja) — voice guide

The Japanese-speaking population in the Bay Area includes both Japanese-Americans (multi-generational) and more recent immigrants. The voice should read as **warm polite** — using 丁寧語 (teineigo, polite form) but **not** the stiff humble keigo register. The brand is a small family operation, not a corporation.

## Tone

Warm, plainspoken, professional but never bureaucratic. Stiff keigo (謙譲語 / 尊敬語) reads as corporate-distant — wrong for Don and Melissa's neighborly business voice. Use です・ます consistently. Reserve heavier honorifics only for the consent block, where formality signals legal seriousness.

## Address form

- Refer to the customer as **お客様** (okyaku-sama) — the warm-polite standard.
- Refer to ourselves as **私たち** (watashi-tachi) or simply imply the subject. Avoid the corporate **弊社** (heisha) — too formal.
- Imperatives soften with **〜ください** (please): "お電話ください" (please call), not the brusque "お電話を".
- Use the brand name "Peninsula Pick Ups" as-is in body copy. Never transliterate to katakana ("ペニンスラ・ピックアップス") — for English brand names, the original Roman lettering reads more authentic.

## Vocabulary

- **"Junk removal"** → **不用品回収** (fuyou-hin kaishuu — collection of unneeded items) is the standard service-business term. Far more natural than ゴミ収集 which implies municipal trash collection.
- **"Hauling"** → **運搬** (unpan — transport) for general; 引っ越し (hikkoshi) is moving — don't confuse.
- **"Cleanout"** → **片付け** (katadzuke — tidying / putting in order). Warm and human.
- **"Pickup"** as a service action → **回収** (kaishuu — collection). Keep "Peninsula Pick Ups" verbatim.
- **"Free quote"** → **無料見積もり** (muryou mitsumori) — standard.
- **"Same-day service"** → **当日対応** (toujitsu taiou — same-day response).
- **"Family-owned"** → **家族経営** (kazoku keiei — family management). Direct and warm.
- **"Licensed and insured"** → **認可・保険完備** (ninka, hoken kanbi — licensed and fully insured). Crisp.

## What to avoid

- **Excessive keigo** in marketing copy. "お電話いただけますと幸いでございます" is press-conference Japanese. "お電話ください" is right.
- **Direct calques from English idioms**. "Real owners. Real phone. Real results." needs a Japanese rhythm: 本物のオーナー、本物の電話、確かな結果 (with full-width comma 、 separating clauses).
- **Half-width punctuation** in body copy. Japanese uses 、 and 。 — never `,` and `.`.
- **Mixing kanji forms**: pick consistent forms. E.g. 出来る vs できる — for friendly copy, prefer hiragana.

## Trust phrases (locked translations)

- "Licensed and insured" → 認可・保険完備
- "Family owned" → 家族経営
- "Locally owned" → 地域密着
- "Verified business line" → 認証済み事業者番号
- "Free quote" → 無料見積もり
