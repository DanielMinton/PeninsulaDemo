# Simplified Chinese (zh-Hans) — voice guide

The Bay Area Chinese-speaking population includes both Mainland-origin and Taiwan-origin readers. Simplified script (zh-Hans) is the safer default for general-audience marketing copy because most younger readers — including those of Taiwan heritage — read it fluently. Traditional script (zh-Hant) would only be added if Daniel decides to fork.

## Tone

Warm, plainspoken, confident. Not corporate. Avoid the stiff government-style register (官方风格) — this is a small family business addressing neighbors. The voice should feel like a trusted neighbor recommending someone, not a brochure.

## Address form

- Use **您** (yǒu, polite "you") consistently. Switching to **你** (nǐ, informal) reads as too familiar from a service business to a stranger.
- Imperatives are direct but framed as service offers, not commands. "请致电..." not "致电..." — the 请 (qǐng) softens to "please call" rather than "call us."

## Vocabulary

- **"Junk removal"** → 垃圾清运 (lājī qīngyùn) is the closest direct translation. "废品回收" (fèipǐn huíshōu) skews toward recycling/scrap. Use 垃圾清运 for the core service. For marketing emphasis, 杂物清运 (záwù qīngyùn — "sundries removal") is also acceptable.
- **"Hauling"** → 搬运 (bānyùn) is generic; 货物搬运 specifies cargo. For trucking-specific work, 运输 works.
- **"Cleanout"** → 清理 (qīnglǐ) for general; 整体清理 for whole-property.
- **"Pickup"** as a brand action → keep "Peninsula Pick Ups" verbatim (do not translate the brand name); when referring to the service, 上门收取 (shàngmén shōuqǔ — door-to-door pickup) reads naturally.
- **"Free quote"** → 免费报价 (miǎnfèi bàojià). Standard, no surprises.
- **"Same-day service"** → 当天上门服务. The 当天 (same-day) prefix reads more naturally than 同日.
- **"Family-owned"** → 家庭经营 (jiātíng jīngyíng — family-operated) is the conventional translation. "家族企业" sounds too large/dynastic for a 2-person operation.
- **"Licensed and insured"** → 持证经营，全险保障 (chí zhèng jīngyíng, quán xiǎn bǎozhàng — operates with license, fully insured). Strong, business-grade phrasing.

## What to avoid

- **Overly literary chéngyǔ (4-character idioms)** in headlines. They make modern marketing copy feel old-fashioned in this register.
- **Direct calques from English idioms**. "Real owners. Real phone. Real results." needs reframing — "真实的老板。真实的电话。真实的服务。" preserves rhythm but the parallelism feels forced. Consider "真人接听，真人服务，真实可靠。" — natural and rhythmic.
- **Mainland-specific bureaucratic terms** that don't apply outside China — e.g. 注册 (zhùcè) for "registered" implies PRC registration. Use 持牌 or 取得执照.
- **Punctuation**: use full-width punctuation (，。、！？) not half-width (,.!?). Crucial for native-feeling typography.

## Trust phrases (locked translations)

- "Licensed and insured" → 持证经营，全险保障
- "Family owned" → 家庭经营
- "Locally owned" → 本地经营
- "Verified business line" → 经核实的业务专线
- "Free quote" → 免费报价

## Phone numbers and brand names

- Phone number `(650) 201-1543` renders LTR exactly as `(650) 201-1543`. Never converted to Chinese number-words.
- "Peninsula Pick Ups" stays in English in body copy (a known foreign brand name reads more authentically than transliteration). The owner names "Don" and "Melissa" also stay in English.
- Cities: "San Carlos" stays in English (well-known SF Peninsula place name). For very long-form copy, an introductory parenthetical translation may help: "圣卡洛斯 (San Carlos)" once on first mention, then English-only thereafter.
